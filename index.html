About Surma River

On the southern slopes of the Naga-Manipur watershed, the Surma River rises as the barak. Within the Indian state of Assam's Cachhar district, the Barak splits into two branches. The northern branch of Surma flows southwest before turning west towards Sylhet. After leaving Sylhet, it runs in a northwesterly and western direction to Sunamganj Town. From there, it flows south to Madna, where it merges with the Kushiyara, the second branch of the Barak. From the Meghalaya Plateau, various rivers and streams fall into it. They are the Lubha, Hari (Kushia), Goyain Gang (Chengar Khal), piyain, Bogapani, Jadukata, Shomeshwari, and kangsa, listed in order from east to west.

Importance of Water Quality

River contamination has grown to be a serious issue. Now, aquatic life is affected. It is now a problem that many fish species are dying and going extinct. Additionally disappearing is the seafloor vegetation. These elements, together with the contaminated river water, have a significant impact on our daily lives and can lead to serious issues. Water Quality Index Prediction and Classification model with the use of machine learning can be easily used to forecast the river's water quality index at future points. These forecasts will aid the monitoring organisations in taking prompt action to improve the water quality.
